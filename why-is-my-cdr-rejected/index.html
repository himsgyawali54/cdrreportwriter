Why is My CDR rejected?

There could be many factors and reasons why your CDR was rejected or could be rejected. Nowadays, the rejection rate is very high compared to the previous number as they check each component of the Career episodes minutely.

Several factors could make an impact on whether your CDR would be positively assessed or negatively assessed. Some of them are explained in detail below:

  1. Copied content:You shouldn’t copy the content from anywhere. EA strictly checks about plagiarism, and you can be banned for up to 12 months if copied from anywhere. Plagiarism is unfaithful and gets your CDR rejected. Most of the work is copied from google/website, and some use friends’ so there is a high chance of plagiarism.
  2. Content on the career episode not as per your Experience/Education: One of the major reasons for the rejection from EA is the lack of explanation of the specific topic. Instead of describing the project, they give details of the team. It means that you should only focus on your work/report. Most of the time, clients are confused about what to write and what kind of project needs to be selected.
  3. Career episodes are too technical: Sometimes, the writing can create a problem. They tend to write everything in a very specialized format. Engineers Australia wants details of how you did those engineering activities and how you worked on that project to prove yourself to be an engineer in that field.
  4. Using the first individual: Specific articulations in your Career Episode are expressly mentioned in the MSA guide. When thinking about group activities or collaboration, you lose focus on your specific job work. ‘I did…’ rather than ‘We did…’ discussion. For example, you may start your sentences by saying “I arranged” or “I planned,” then go on to say “I recorded” or “I set up” or something like that. This is also one of the rejection reasons for the CDR.
  5. Too long Career Episode: Career Episodes should be between 1,000 and 2,500 words long: As long as you adhere to the specified word limit, don’t forget to include all of EA’s specifics. We recommend you start by reading the EA guidelines and the ANZSCO Code examples, then decide which areas of your professional development will best demonstrate your specialized skills.
  6. Unclear Summary Statement: Before you begin writing the CDR report, you should realize that it includes three key components: a Summary Statement, three Career Episodes, and a list of Continuous Professional Development (CPD). Each one plays a crucial function in the CDR report. The summary statement is an important aspect of the CDR since it provides cross-references for all career episodes. The majority of assessors read the first page. It demonstrates how well you process information, which gives people their first impressions of you.
  7. Unusual project selection: You may face CDR rejection because your supporting project was insufficient. Engineers Australia may reject your proposal if you present it unreasonably. While writing a CDR report, You should choose an appropriate project based on your educational qualifications.
  8. Organizational structure flaws: The organizational structure should clearly define your hierarchy and position in the project. It is critical to convey your precise position to properly describe the work you have done in your project as viable in the capacity occupied.
  9. The CDR suffers from a lack of creative design: Design activities are given top priority in CDR evaluation. When you include technical design activities in your CDR report, you increase the likelihood of it being positively evaluated. Similarly, a lack of creative design may impede a positive assessment of your CDR report. As a result, explain your technological experience and innovative engineering about your designing activities, and highlight them as much as possible.
  10. Not mentioning the problematic statements: In your career episode report, it is critical to note the problems and the steps you took to solve them. Mentioning problem statements in your account clarifies exactly what you contributed and achieved during the project. As a result, make certain that each of your reports contains all the problem statements and solutions.
