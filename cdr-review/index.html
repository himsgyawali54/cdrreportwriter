Frequently Asked Questions

ENGINEERS AUSTRALIA COMMENTS WE HAVE HANDLED

Competency demonstration report (CDR) is the pathway for Engineers whose degree/qualifications don’t fall into the direct accreditation category of Engineers’ Australia. Suppose you are a graduate engineer accredited by the Australian Education wing (Sydney Accord, Washington Accord, Dublin Accord). In that case, you don’t need a CDR for registering yourself as a registered engineer in Australia. Else, CDR is the only pathway for you to register yourself as a registered engineer in Australia.

The following documents are required for preparing a complete CDR application:

  • One Passport Size Photograph
  • Bio Page of Identity Proof
  • CV / Resume
  • English Proficiency Certificate (IELTS, TOEFL iBT, and PTE)
  • Certificate of Graduation
  • Certified Academic Transcripts
  • 3 Career Episodes
  • Summary Statement
  • Continuing Professional Development (CPD)

If you do not meet the requirements for your nominated occupation, you will be sent a letter stating that you did not complete the pre-migration skills assessment. The assessment components where your application did not meet the requirements will be indicated in the letter. If you don’t agree with the outcome of your application, you are highly recommended to talk to the assessor, who will then recommend you the available options for a reassessment.
