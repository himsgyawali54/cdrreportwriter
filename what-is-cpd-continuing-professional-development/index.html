What is CPD Engineers Australia?

Continuing Professional Development or CPD Engineers Australia is prepared to help engineers keep their engineering qualifications current and updated. It involves learning, being engaged in regular career development activities, and professional networking. CPD aims to help engineers improve their engineering skills, and maintain their professional engineering status.

CPD Hour Requirements to be Fulfilled

There are two credit hour requirements that have been based on Chartered and NER credentials. You should have 150 hours of professional activities maintained every 3 years to keep these credentials.

Quick Guide to writing CPD for Engineer Australia

Engineer's Australia has outlined the requirements for a CPD Report, and how it should be structured:

  • It should be provided in a list format (chronological order)
  • The report should contain details of any formal or informal engineering activity undertaken.
  • The report must not exceed one page.
  • It is not necessary to attach certificates for courses or events participated in.

Note: CPD should also include tasks performed and must include certifications too. You can always Contact Us if you are confused and need a helping hand.

Engineers Australia CPD

Engineers Australia offers its members the opportunity to access a complete Program of Continuous Professional Development (CPD) through their membership. The program includes access to tertiary and short courses, a collection of online resources such as training, and webinars, as well as a comprehensive calendar of events featuring the latest engineering seminars, conferences, and technical presentations.

What is the Engineers Australia CPD Policy?

Engineers Australia has laid out a Continuing Professional Development (CPD) policy for its members. This is also for those seeking Chartered status, or those registered with the National Engineers Register. It includes that all members who have been part of Engineers Australia for at least 5 years must complete 150 hours of CPD activities in the past year. It also states that those who are working part-time or have taken a career break in the last 3 years do not need to comply. This policy helps engineers to improve their careers and gain knowledge and skills.

CPD Record-Keeping

Professional Engineers must save documents showing that they have completed the Continuing Professional Development (CPD) requirements for each year and keep these records for a minimum of 5 years. These documents should be accompanied by supporting evidence of their activities, such as certificates of completion issued by an RTO, TAFE, or an Australian university.

They should also include documentation of attendance that has the following things mentioned:

  • Professional Engineer's name
  • Title and dates of the course/training
  • Duration
  • Teacher's name
  • Name of the training organisation.

In the case of receiving a request in writing, the engineer must provide copies of their CPD records.

Benefits of CPD

  • CPD increases knowledge and expertise related to the engineering sector.
  • It improves technical skills along with enhancing academic knowledge.
  • It provides more job prospects in the engineering field.
  • Makes you competitive in the job market.

Engineer Australia CPD Assessments

After the CPD audit, the CPD auditor must determine whether the applicant has fulfilled the CPD requirements satisfactorily or not. However, before assessing insufficient submission, the auditor must notify the applicant of the proposed assessment, including the reasons for it, and allow them at least 10 business days to comment on it.

Engineers Australia will then inform the member of any insufficient assessment and provide details of their right to request a review of the assessment. This review must be done in writing and include enough information to allow it to be investigated properly. It should also specify that the CPD auditor did not take relevant material into account, took irrelevant material, failed to follow due process, or demonstrated bias against the member. If the member's chartered status or NER registration has been revoked due to the CPD audit, they have 90 days to apply for a review.
