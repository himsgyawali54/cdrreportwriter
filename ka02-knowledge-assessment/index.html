KA02 Assessment for Engineers Migrating to New Zealand via IPENZ

Our team of expert engineers can provide premium KA02 writing services to help you obtain a positive skill assessment from IPENZ.

KA02 is a document required if an applicant is willing to migrate to New Zealand as an Engineer or for any Engineering field. It helps assess an applicant's skills and is submitted by those who do not hold any ICT qualification but are willing to migrate and work professionally in the ICT field.

To evaluate the knowledge which is necessary to support engineering qualifications, applicants should demonstrate their commitment to ethical and professional responsibilities. Applicants are also required to show their understanding of the relevant research in the field. This evaluation consists of five sections. The evaluation process provides an opportunity for candidates to show their expertise and understanding of the engineering field. Applicants should also provide proof of their work on the project by submitting all relevant documents, emails, or files. This is necessary since IPENZ's competence process requires evidence.

Eligibility for KA02 Assessment

Certain eligibility criteria should be followed for the KA02 Assessment.

  • If the university from which you are applying is not accredited then you must submit the KA02 anyhow.
  • Having a degree of 4 years if engineering is a must.
  • Submit a knowledge profile made up of twelve components, including context statements and performance indicators.
  • Competency-based evaluation is a must to ensure that the applicant has fulfilled every step of the evaluation.
  • Candidates should include engineering-related models, statistics, and designs in their projects to demonstrate their competence.

What are the ways to write a KA02 report?

There are three steps you need to follow while writing a KA02 Report. They are:

  1. Knowledge Profile
  2. Evidence of Application of Knowledge
  3. Supplementary Evidence

1. Knowledge Profile

You will be evaluated and your engineering capacity will be tested here. You must also demonstrate your mathematical understanding of the engineering field. You must also summarise key engineering fundamentals. Focus on the more progressive elements of work you did when describing how your educational program contributed to your development.

2. Evidence of Application of Knowledge

In this section, you need to show your expertise in engineering, which is focused on the theoretical aspect. A detailed overview of the project should include the purpose, time, specific duties, and your role in the organisation. You must include examples of your work such as calculations, reports, or analyses that you have personally completed. This will help to verify your engineering knowledge, skills, and abilities.

3. Supplementary Evidence

In this section, you must present a certified copy of your academic transcript that lists your accomplishments in chronological order. You should also provide a detailed summary of your work experience as well as any other activities that have contributed to your advancement in the engineering field. Additional courses, seminars, workshops, or any other relevant activities that have helped you gain a deeper understanding of your chosen engineering field could be included.

Types of KA02 Knowledge Assessment report

To be considered for membership, candidates must submit a Knowledge Assessment Report that compares their qualifications to the current qualifications for professional engineers in New Zealand.

A specific type of Knowledge Assessment Report must be submitted to the Institution of Professional Engineers New Zealand depending on the origin of the candidate's qualification (IPENZ).

  • If the candidate obtained their qualification from a Washington Accord-accredited university or institution, they must submit a KA01 report.
  • If the candidate has graduated from a university or institution that is not a member of the Washington Accord or another Australian university, they must submit a KA02 report.

Both types of Knowledge Assessment Reports must provide an accurate comparison of the candidate's qualifications to New Zealand's current qualifications standards for professional engineers.

Potential Problems That Can be Faced By The Applicants

  • Applicants should ensure that they have good language skills as the language barrier could be a potential issue.
  • You cannot submit your KA02 report once it has been rejected.
  • IPENZ is very cautious when it comes to reviewing your application. Make sure that your report is unique and the documents are well organised as small mistakes can lead to rejection.

Things to Consider Before Writing a KA02 Report

  • Applicants should be able to demonstrate the necessary skills and knowledge.
  • You need to ensure that you have a quality report.
  • The reports should be free of any plagiarism as the authority can find out plagiarised content very easily.
  • To support their achievement, each applicant must detail a few tasks they have completed.
  • Applicants should submit their reports on time as there is a time limit for submitting the KA02.
  • The report should include calculations, charts, and sections about work episodes.
  • If certain files are unavailable, you must prepare them as well since the authority can ask for additional documents anytime.
